About Me

Hello , I’m Rob, a self-taught front end developer from Maryland. I have worked in the public health research field for over 4 years. The public health field has always had a special place in my heart, but having time away from the office, it was time to explore something new.

Looking to make a career change, I have always had a passion to pursue a career in the tech industry in some fashion. Since the pandemic started, I have continued to work my full time job remotely. I started learning about UX/UI design, and I have completed a few projects over the past year. While learning about UX design, I learned how having a developer background would be beneficial in the design process. From research I discovered that outside of the traditional path to becoming a web developer, the next best option was to attend a boot camp. Not that I couldn't commit to the time to complete the camp, my issue was I couldn’t afford to pay for the camp along with my student loans and other financial obligations. I did some more research and with the help of many sources, I created an outline to become a self-taught front end developer.

Using various teaching websites and resources I started learning how to code. Over the next few months, I used mornings, lunch breaks, nights and any free time I could spare learning how to code. During this time I also created projects with HTML, CSS and vanilla Javascript. Once I became confident in the basics, I started to learn React js and other front end technologies.

When I'm not coding, I enjoy watching TV, playing videogames, and discovering local craft beers.
